Investment: Something that is purchased with the hope that it will generate income or will appreciate in the future.

Stock Market: The collection of markets and exchanges where the issuing and trading of equities (stocks of publicly held companies), bonds and other sorts of securities takes place.

Stock: A stock is a share in the ownership of a company. Stock represents a claim on the company's assets and earnings.

Dividend: A dividend is a distribution of a portion of a company's earnings, decided by the board of directors, to a class of its shareholders.

Volume: The total quantity of shares or contracts traded for a specified security

P/E Ratio: (Price-earnings ratio) The ratio for valuing a company that measures its current share price relative to its per-share earnings

Div/Yield: A financial ratio that indicates how much a company pays out in dividends each year relative to its share price.
